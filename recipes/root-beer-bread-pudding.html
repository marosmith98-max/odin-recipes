Root Beer Bread Pudding

Description of the recipe

This root beer bread pudding is a comfort-food dessert that blends the warm, spiced sweetness of root beer with custard‑soaked bread.

The soda adds caramel and vanilla notes that echo classic diner floats, while the baked pudding turns rich and tender, perfect for topping with a swirl of whipped cream.

Ingredients

  • 6 cups of cubed day-old bread (French bread works great)
  • 3 cups of root beer
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup raisins (optional)
  • powdered sugar for dusting (optional)
  • vanilla ice cream or whipped cream for serving (optional)

Steps to prepare

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish. Place the cubed bread in a large mixing bowl.

  2. Whisk together the root beer, heavy cream, sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt in a separate bowl until well combined.

  3. Pour the root beer mixture over the bread cubes, making sure all the bread is soaked. Let it sit for about 10 minutes, allowing the bread to absorb the mixture.

  4. If you're using raisins, sprinkle them evenly over the soaked bread mixture and gently stir to combine. Pour the soaked bread mixture into the greased baking dish, spreading it out evenly.

  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and a knife inserted into the center comes out clean.

  6. Remove from the oven and let it cool slightly before serving. Dust with powdered sugar before serving.
