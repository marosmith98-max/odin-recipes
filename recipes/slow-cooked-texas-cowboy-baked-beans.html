Slow-Cooked Texas Cowboy Baked Beans

Description of the recipe

Texas Cowboy Baked Beans are the kind of hearty, smoky side dish that feels right at home in Amarillo, where the Big Texan Steak Ranch has been serving up oversized Western hospitality since 1960.

Founded by R.J. “Bob” Lee along Route 66, the restaurant became famous for its 72‑ounce steak challenge—a 4.5‑pound Texas King served with shrimp cocktail, baked potato, salad, and a roll.

Finish it all in under an hour, and the meal is free.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 4 (16 ounce) cans baked beans with pork
  • 1 (4 ounce can) green chile peppers
  • 1 small Vidalia onion, chopped
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce (go for a smoky Texas-style one!)
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 3 tablespoons hot pepper sauce (or to taste)
  • cooked bacon or sliced smoked sausage for extra richness

Steps to prepare

  1. Cook ground beef in a skillet over medium heat until it is no longer pink, about 5 minutes. Drain excess fat.

  2. In a slow cooker (or large pot), combine browned beef, baked beans, green chiles, onion, and barbecue sauce.

  3. Stir in brown sugar, garlic powder, chili powder, and hot pepper sauce.

  4. If using a slow cooker, set to high for 2 hours or low for 4-5 hours. If using a stovetop, simmer on low for about 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
